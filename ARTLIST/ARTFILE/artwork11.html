About Artwork

Cafe Terrace at Night

Vincent Van Gogh

1888

*Cafe Terrace at Night* by Vincent van Gogh, painted in 1888 in Arles, France, depicts a warmly lit café under a starry evening sky. The scene shows people dining and strolling along a cobblestone street, illuminated by the golden glow of the café’s lanterns that contrast beautifully with the deep blues and purples of the night. Van Gogh used bold colors and expressive brushstrokes to capture both the serenity and vibrancy of nighttime life, creating a sense of warmth and human connection within the quiet of the city. The painting reflects his fascination with light, atmosphere, and the emotional depth found in ordinary moments.

About Artist

Vincent Van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh (1853–1890) was the Dutch Post-Impressionist master who was characterized by his unique use of color and expressive and textured brushwork (impasto), which he used to convey intense emotion and personal feeling rather than just a simple representation of reality.
