Starry Night Over the Rhone

Vincent Van Gogh

1888

Starry Night Over the Rhône is famous for the scene that captures the interplay between natural starlight and artificial light. This painting is one of Van Gogh's several "night effects" works. It depicts the view of the Rhône River at night from the bank in Arles, France, a short walk from the Yellow House where Van Gogh was living at the time.

About Artist

Vincent Van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh (1853–1890) was a Dutch Post-Impressionist painter who became one of the most famous and influential figures in Western art history. He is known for his vivid colors, textured brushwork (impasto), and emotional expressiveness. He used color and brushstrokes not just to represent reality but to convey his emotions and internal world.
