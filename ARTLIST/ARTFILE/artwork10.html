About Artwork

Blue and Green Music

Georgia O’Keeffe

1921

Blue and Green Music by Georgia O’Keeffe, painted between 1919 and 1921, is an abstract work that visually expresses the rhythm and harmony of music through color and form. Using flowing shapes and layered tones of blue and green, O’Keeffe creates a sense of movement and energy that mimics the experience of listening to a melody. The painting reflects her fascination with synesthesia the blending of senses and her belief that visual art, like music, can evoke emotion without depicting real objects. It stands as one of her most innovative explorations of translating sound into visual beauty.

About Artist

Georgia O’Keeffe

Georgia O’Keeffe (1887-1986) was an American modernist painter known for her large-scale flower paintings, New Mexico landscapes, and abstract works. A pioneer of American modernism, she explored themes of nature, form, and color with bold compositions that challenged traditional artistic conventions.
