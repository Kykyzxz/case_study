The Starry Night

Vincent Van Gogh

1889

The Starry Night by Vincent van Gogh, painted in 1889 during his stay at the Saint-Paul-de-Mausole asylum, portrays a swirling night sky over a quiet village. Dominated by vibrant blues and luminous yellows, the painting captures a dramatic contrast between the turbulent heavens and the calm landscape below. Van Gogh’s expressive brushstrokes and use of impasto create a sense of movement and emotion, symbolizing both the beauty and chaos of nature. The towering cypress tree connects earth and sky, reflecting themes of spirituality and longing. More than just a depiction of a night scene, the artwork embodies Van Gogh’s emotional intensity and his search for hope and meaning amidst turmoil.

About Artist

Vincent Van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh (1853-1890) was a Dutch Post-Impressionist known for his vibrant colors and emotional depth. Despite personal struggles, he created masterpieces that later became symbols of passion and artistic genius.
