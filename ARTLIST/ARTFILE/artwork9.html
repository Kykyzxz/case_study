About Artwork

A Sunday on La Grande Jatte

Georges Seurat

1884–1886

A Sunday Afternoon on the Island of La Grande Jatte by Georges Seurat, painted between 1884 and 1886, portrays Parisians leisurely spending a sunny afternoon along the Seine River. Using the innovative technique of pointillism tiny dots of pure color that visually blend from a distance Seurat creates a luminous and harmonious scene that captures both the tranquility and structure of modern urban life. The carefully balanced composition, with its mix of stillness and subtle movement, reflects Seurat’s interest in order, color theory, and the relationship between people and their environment. The painting remains a defining masterpiece of the Neo-Impressionist movement.

About Artist

Georges Seurat

Georges-Pierre Seurat (1859–1891) was a French Post-Impressionist painter known for a highly systematic and scientific approach to art. He devised the technique of Pointillism (he preferred the term Divisionism), which involves applying tiny, distinct dots of pure color in patterns.
