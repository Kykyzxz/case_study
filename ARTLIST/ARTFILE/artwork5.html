About Artwork

Nocturne: Blue and Gold - Old Battersea Bridge

James Abbott McNeill Whistler

1872-1875

Painted around 1872-75 by James Abbott McNeill Whistler, Nocturne: Blue and Gold – Old Battersea Bridge depicts the old wooden Battersea Bridge spanning the River Thames at dusk. Whistler renders the scene in a subdued palette of deep blues and muted golds, emphasizing atmosphere over precise detail. A towering pier of the bridge looms in the foreground, silhouetted against a softly glowing sky and shimmering water, while distant lights and subtle fireworks create a fleeting sparkle. The work is emblematic of Whistler’s “nocturne” series—paintings named like musical compositions, intended to evoke mood rather than narrate a story. In this piece he intentionally distorted scale and perspective (for example, exaggerating the height of the bridge support) and drew inspiration from Japanese wood-cut aesthetics, to create a more poetic, abstracted vision of the landscape.

About Artist

James Abbott McNeill Whistler

James Abbott McNeill Whistler (1843-1903) was an American-born artist and a leading figure in the Aesthetic movement. Known for his “art for art’s sake” philosophy, he pioneered the nocturne series, emphasizing atmospheric tonal harmonies over detailed representation.
