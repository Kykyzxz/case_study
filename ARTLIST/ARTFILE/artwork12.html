About Artwork

The Scream

Edvard Munch

1893

The Scream by Edvard Munch, created in 1893, is an iconic expressionist painting that portrays a figure standing on a bridge, clutching its face in terror while emitting a silent cry beneath a swirling, blood-red sky. The distorted lines, intense colors, and dramatic composition convey overwhelming emotion and psychological anguish. Inspired by Munch’s own feelings of anxiety and existential dread, the artwork captures a universal sense of fear and isolation in the modern world. Through its raw emotion and striking visual power, The Scream remains one of the most powerful representations of human despair in art history.

About Artist

Edvard Munch

Edvard Munch (1863–1944) was a Norwegian painter and printmaker whose work was foundational to the Symbolist and Expressionist movements. Munch's art was deeply personal and rooted in his own psychological struggles and the tragedies of his life. He aimed to depict "what the human being has felt in relation to it," a key tenet of Symbolism.
