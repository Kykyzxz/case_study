Impressionism, Sunrise

Claude Monet

1872

ChatGPT said: Impression, Sunrise by Claude Monet, painted in 1872, depicts the harbor of Le Havre at dawn, where the orange sun glows through misty blue-gray tones of sky and water. Using quick, loose brushstrokes and soft color contrasts, Monet captures the fleeting effects of light and atmosphere rather than detailed forms, allowing boats and buildings to blend into the haze. The painting embodies the essence of modern Impressionism—focusing on momentary perception and emotional response—and famously gave the movement its name after critics mocked its unfinished, “impressionistic” style. Today, it stands as a defining work that revolutionized how artists portrayed light, color, and mood.

About Artist

Claude Monet

Claude Monet (1840-1926) was a French painter and founder of Impressionism. He dedicated his career to capturing the effects of light and atmosphere through loose brushwork and vibrant color, often painting the same subjects at different times to observe changing conditions.
