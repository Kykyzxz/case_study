About Artwork

Pool with Two Figures

David Hockney

1972

Portrait of an Artist (Pool with Two Figures) by David Hockney, painted in 1972, depicts two men in a serene poolside settingone standing fully clothed at the edge, looking down, and the other swimming underwater. Created from the combination of two separate photographs, the artwork contrasts stillness and movement, clarity and distortion. Its bright, flat colors and clean composition reflect Hockney’s signature style, inspired by the light and leisure of California. The piece is often interpreted as a reflection of Hockney’s personal emotions, possibly representing his former lover, and explores themes of intimacy, distance, and observation. It remains one of Hockney’s most celebrated works, capturing both visual beauty and emotional complexity.

About Artist

David Hockney

David Hockney (born 1937) is a British contemporary artist known for his vibrant use of color and innovative depictions of modern life. A leading figure in Pop Art, his works often explore themes of identity, relationships, and perception.
