About Artwork

At the Moulin Rogue: The Dance

Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec

1890

At the Moulin Rouge: The Dance by Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec, painted around 1889–1890, vividly portrays the lively nightlife of Paris’s famous cabaret, the Moulin Rouge. The painting centers on the dancer Valentin le Désossé twirling with a partner amid a crowd of elegantly dressed patrons enjoying the energetic atmosphere. With bold outlines, tilted perspectives, and striking contrasts of light and color, Toulouse-Lautrec captures both the excitement and decadence of late 19th-century Parisian entertainment. The artwork reflects the artist’s fascination with urban life, dance, and the fleeting, dynamic spirit of the modern age.

About Artist

Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec

Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec (1864-1901) was a French painter and printmaker known for documenting the bohemian nightlife of late 19th-century Paris. His distinctive style featured bold colors, simplified forms, and keen observation of human character, particularly in depictions of cabarets, theaters, and dance halls.
