About Artwork

The Blue Vase

Paul Cézanne

1885-1887

The Blue Vase by Paul Cézanne is a still-life composition featuring a blue vase of flowers surrounded by fruit, bottles, and dishes arranged on a tabletop. Rather than focusing on realistic detail, Cézanne emphasizes the balance of color, form, and structure—using the dominant blue tone to unify the entire scene. Through soft brushwork, subtle light, and carefully arranged shapes, he transforms ordinary objects into a study of harmony and depth. The painting reflects Cézanne’s revolutionary approach to still life, bridging traditional realism and the emerging modern art movement.

About Artist

Paul Cézanne

Paul Cézanne (1839–1906) was a highly influential French painter who is often referred to as the "father of modern art" for bridging the gap between late 19th-century Impressionism and early 20th-century movements such as Cubism and Fauvism. He believed that all natural forms could be reduced to basic geometric shapes.
