Post - Impressionism

Starry Night Over the Rhone

Vincent Van Gogh

1888

Starry Night Over the Rhône is famous for the scene that captures the interplay between natural starlight and artificial light. This painting is one of Van Gogh's several "night effects" works. It depicts the view of the Rhône River at night from the bank in Arles, France, a short walk from the Yellow House where Van Gogh was living at the time.

Renaissance

Mona Lisa

Leonardo da Vinci

1503-1506

The Mona Lisa is one of the most famous paintings in the world, known for its mysterious smile and lifelike portrayal. It depicts a woman with an enigmatic expression, seated against a distant, dreamlike landscape. Leonardo’s mastery of sfumato—the delicate blending of tones and colors gives the portrait a soft, realistic quality.

Post - Impressionism

The Starry Night

Vincent Van Gogh

1889

The Starry Night depicts a swirling night sky over a quiet village, showcasing Van Gogh’s bold colors and expressive brushstrokes. Painted from his asylum room, it reflects his emotions and fascination with nature and the cosmos.

Pop Art

Pool with Two Figures

David Hockney

1972

Pool with Two Figures shows a man swimming underwater while another stands poolside, gazing down. The painting contrasts movement and stillness, capturing emotion through bright colours and clean composition.

Tonalism

Nocturne: Blue and Gold - Old Battersea Bridge

James Abbott McNeill Whistler

1872-1875

This nocturne depicts Old Battersea Bridge spanning the Thames River at night in atmospheric blues and golds. A solitary figure stands on the bridge while golden fireworks illuminate the dusky sky, creating a dreamlike version of the London riverscape.

Impressionism

Impressionism, Sunrise

Claude Monet

1872

Impression, Sunrise depicts the port of Le Havre at dawn, with the sun casting an orange glow over misty waters. Small boats appear as dark silhouettes against the hazy atmosphere. The painting’s loose brushwork and focus on capturing fleeting light gave the Impressionist movement its name.

Post - Impressionism

At the Moulin Rouge: The Dance

Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec

1890

At the Moulin Rouge: The Dance captures the lively atmosphere of the famous Parisian nightclub, showing dancers and patrons in motion. The composition features bold outlines and flattened forms, with dynamic figures set against the vibrant interior of the dance hall.

Post - Impressionism

The Blue Vase

Paul Cézanne

1885-1887

The Blue Vase depicts a simple blue ceramic vase filled with flowers set on a table. Cézanne employs his characteristic approach of breaking down forms into geometric shapes and using multiple perspectives.

Neo - Impressionism

A Sunday on La Grande Jatte

Georges Seurat

1884–1886

A Sunday on La Grande Jatte is a monumental oil painting and a foundational piece of the Neo-Impressionist movement. The painting captures a typical Sunday afternoon in the 1880s on the island of La Grande Jatte, a popular recreational spot for Parisians from different social classes who sought a brief escape from city life.

Abstract Art

Blue and Green Music

Georgia O’Keeffe

1921

Blue and Green Music is an abstract composition featuring flowing, organic forms in shades of blue and green. The painting suggests rhythm and movement through its undulating shapes and color harmonies, reflecting O’Keeffe’s synesthetic response to music and her exploration of abstraction as visual melody.

Post - Impressionism

Cafe Terrace at Night

Vincent Van Gogh

1889

Café Terrace at Night is a vibrant oil painting that captures the warm, gaslight-illuminated terrace of a café against a deep blue, star-filled sky. This juxtaposition of cozy, artificial light and cool, natural darkness creates visual tension and captures the vibrant atmosphere of the place.

Proto - Expressionism

The Scream

Vincent Van Gogh

1893

The Scream has a jarring palette of fiery oranges and reds for the sky, contrasting with deep blues for the fjord. The agonized, androgynous, skull-shaped figure has become an enduring symbol of modern existential dread and the "anxiety of modern life."
